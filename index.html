Aerobic Fitness
Aerobic Fitness

The ideal fitness program is one that combines resistance training with aerobic exercise. The American Heart Association recommends a minimum of 30 minutes of cardiovascular activity 5 to 7 days a week with intensity appropriate to your aerobic conditioning. The intensity of the exercise is determined by your goals, your limitations and your current fitness level.

Flexibility
Flexibility

Flexibility is the ability of a joint or series of joints to move through an unrestricted, pain free range of motion. Active flexibility could be considered a workout in itself, as it is activating muscles in a way that is similar to yoga. It can be done before or after physical activity or as a stand-alone workout. The benefits of flexibility include injury prevention, enhanced athletic performance and better posture and balance.

Stability and Balance
Stability and Balance

Balance and stability training are often overlooked when developing a fitness program. Balance is a key component in fitness along with strength, endurance and flexibility. Some of the benefits include: body awareness, coordination, joint flexibility, faster reaction time and long-term health. Improving balance can also assist with performing your everyday activities like standing on your tiptoes, walking on uneven ground, participating in sports or throwing a ball with your kids.
